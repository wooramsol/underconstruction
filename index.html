Butane gas canisters are among the most common types of gas containers, but recycling them is often inconvenient‚Äîand sometimes even dangerous. To recycle them safely, a hole must be punctured to release pressure and any remaining gas, making the metal safe for disposal. Most people need to carry a separate tool for this, but even that small inconvenience can discourage proper recycling. This concept proposes a simple solution: a built-in metal clip in the canister itself. Inexpensive to manufacture and easy to use, the clip punctures the can when struck against a flat surface.

A container for capturing human breath. This balloon can be inflated and kept as a keepsake or given as a gift to commemorate a special moment. BREATH offers a unique way to preserve fleeting memories. Made entirely from thermoplastic polyurethane, it holds up to 500 millilitres of air, which is roughly the average volume exhaled in a single breath by an adult. Users can write directly on the balloon to note whose breath it contains and the date it was exhaled, preserving it for posterity. Crowdfunding attempt on Kickstarter was unsuccessful.
